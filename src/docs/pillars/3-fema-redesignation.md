## Overview

Under **FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act)** regulations, NRIs are **required** to convert their NRE/NRO accounts to Resident accounts within a reasonable timeframe after becoming Indian residents. This is not optionalâ€”failure to comply can result in severe penalties.

## Key Action

> **Inform bank within 30-60 days of becoming resident**

## Why It's Mandatory

When you return to India and become a "resident" under FEMA, you are legally required to:
1. Notify your banks about your change in residential status
2. Convert or close your NRI accounts
3. Update all documentation

Continuing to operate NRE/NRO accounts as an NRI when you are actually a resident is a **FEMA violation**.

## Account Conversion Rules

| Account Type | Required Action |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **NRE Savings Account** | Convert to Resident Savings OR transfer to RFC |
| **NRO Savings Account** | Convert to Resident Savings OR close |
| **NRE Fixed Deposits** | Re-designate as Resident FD immediately |
| **NRO Fixed Deposits** | Continue (interest becomes taxable) OR close |
| **FCNR(B) Deposits** | Can hold until maturity, then convert to Resident/RFC |
| **NRI Demat Account** | Close and open new Resident Demat, transfer securities |
| **Mutual Funds** | Update KYC status from NRI to Resident with AMCs |

## Special Considerations

### NRE Fixed Deposits
- Must be re-designated immediately
- Interest earned post-conversion may be taxable as resident
- Can break FD and transfer to RFC if needed

### FCNR Deposits
- Can be held until maturity date
- Upon maturity, must convert to RFC or Resident Rupee account
- Interest remains tax-free until maturity

### Joint Accounts
- If one holder changes status, entire account treatment changes
- All joint holders must update documentation

### Power of Attorney
- Review and update any POAs given for NRI accounts
- POA holder roles may change post-conversion

## FEMA vs Income Tax Residency

**Important**: Residential status can differ under FEMA and Income Tax Act.

| Aspect | FEMA | Income Tax |
|--------|------|------------|
| Determining factor | Intent to stay | Number of days in India |
| When status changes | Upon returning with intent to settle | After meeting day-count threshold |
| Purpose | Foreign exchange compliance | Tax liability |

You could be:
- FEMA Resident but IT Non-Resident (arrived with intent but few days)
- FEMA NRI but IT Resident (frequent visits totaling 182+ days)

**Always consult a professional** to determine your status under both laws.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I keep my NRE account if I plan to go back abroad?
**A**: If you return to India intending to stay permanently, you must convert. "Intent" matters under FEMA. If your stay is genuinely temporary, consult a professional.

### Q: What happens to the interest in my NRE account?
**A**: NRE account interest was tax-free as NRI. Upon conversion, future interest becomes taxable. Consider transferring to RFC for continued tax efficiency during RNOR period.

### Q: Can I convert NRE back to NRI account if I leave again?
**A**: If you become an NRI again (by moving abroad), you can open new NRE/NRO accounts. But you cannot "revert" a converted resident account.
